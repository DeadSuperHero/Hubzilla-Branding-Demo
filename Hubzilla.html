Decentralize. Build a more robust network.

Now open your eyes. We've done some amazing things with decentralization technology over the last few years and have created a different kind of network. We call it the Red Matrix. We're trying to empower individuals to take back the internet from the walled gardens that now dominate our online lives.

The Red Matrix is a network without a central authority, where individuals are in charge. Cloud storage, file sharing, communications, content creation and management belong to you and can be shared (or not shared) as you wish. The Red Matrix software is free and open source; created by volunteers and distributed under the MIT license.

And the Red Matrix has Got Zot.

Your identity is your own. One identity across the network.

Zot is a revolutionary protocol which provides decentralized communications and identity management across the network, powering communication services and social networking. Any channel and its services can make full use of feature-rich social communications on a global scale. These communications may be public or private, where privacy is enforced by a range of encryption techniques from the server level to the user level.

Zot's identity services are unique. It's like OpenID or Persona on steroids. It provides invisible single sign-on across all sites in the network. Plus, your identity is nomadic. If your primary Red Matrix hub is unavailable, your communications will seamlessly reach your contacts via your indentity clones on other hubs. These nomadic instances are kept in sync so any instance can take over if another one is compromised or damaged. You can back up your identity and relationships to your computer, or transfer to a different hub entirely! This amazing capability protects you against system failures and censorship.

As you browse the matrix viewing channels and their unique content, you are seamlessly authenticated as you go, even across completely different server hubs. No password dialogues. Nothing to type. You're just greeted by name on every new site you visit. We call it "magic-auth". You need only log in to your account once to allow you to access any authenticated services provided in the network - such as shopping or access to private information. This is just like the services offered by large corporate providers with huge user databases, but your password isn't stored on a thousand different sites where it can be stolen.

You control your data. Red Matrix enforces your permissions.

Zot allows you to provide fine-grained permissions to any content you wish to publish - and these permissions extend across the Red Matrix. This is like having one super huge website made up of an army of small individual websites - and where each channel in the matrix can completely control their privacy and sharing preferences for any web resources they create. Currently the matrix supports communications, photo albums, events, and WebDAV file storage. The list of features continues to grow. Every object and how it is shared and with whom is completely under your control.
